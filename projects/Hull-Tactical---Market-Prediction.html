Hull Tactical - Market Prediction (Kaggle Competition)

I'm currently participating in the Hull Tactical - Market Prediction competition on Kaggle. The challenge involves forecasting an allocation value between 0 and 2, representing exposure to the S&P 500 versus cash.

The objective is to design a model that dynamically adjusts this allocation based on historical and market-related features, effectively simulating an active portfolio management strategy.

The competition is evaluated using a modified Sharpe ratio, which penalizes models that either take on substantially more volatility than the market or fail to outperform its returns. This metric emphasises risk-adjusted performance, rewarding stable and consistent predictions rather than aggressive or overfit strategies.

My work so far has focussed on:

  • Feature engineering for time-series and market indicators
  • Model experimentation, including ensemble and deep learning approaches
  • Portfolio-simulation techniques to better align predictions with the competition's risk-based metric
Since the competition is still ongoing, my repository is currently private. Once it concludes, I plan to make the code and analysis publically available.
